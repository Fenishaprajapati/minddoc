GENERALIZED ANXIETY DISORDER

GAD is like having a constant worry companion. People with GAD experience excessive, uncontrollable anxiety about everyday things.
Imagine feeling on edge, expecting the worst, even when there's no apparent reason. Physical symptoms like muscle tension and restlessness often tag along.

Symptoms:
  • 1.Persistent Worrying: Overthinking about various aspects of life. 2.Restlessness: Feeling on edge and unable to relax.
  • 3.Muscle Tension: Physical manifestation of stress in the body. 4.Fatigue: Constant worrying can be mentally and physically exhausting.
  • 5.Difficulty Concentrating: The mind is preoccupied with anxious thoughts. 6.Irritability: GAD can make individuals more sensitive to irritants.
  • 7.Sleep Disturbances: Difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep. 8.Excessive Sweating: Physical manifestation of heightened anxiety.
Remedies:
  • -Embrace mindfulness, practice relaxation techniques, exercise regularly, and challenge irrational thoughts.
  • -Mindfulness and Meditation: Techniques to stay present and manage anxious thoughts. -Regular Exercise: Physical activity helps in reducing stress and anxiety.
  • -Challenge Irrational Thoughts: Question the validity of anxious beliefs. -Therapy: Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is particularly effective.
  • -Journaling: Writing down thoughts can help identify patterns and triggers. -Breathing Exercises: Deep, slow breathing to calm the nervous system.
  • -Create a Worry Period: Designate a specific time for worrying to contain anxiety.
Diagnosis:
  • - A mental health professional assesses symptoms and may use specific criteria outlined in the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-5).
