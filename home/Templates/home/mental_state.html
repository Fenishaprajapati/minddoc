    Frequently asked questions..

    - can mental diseases be completely cured or treated ?

    Yes, many mental health conditions can be treated or managed effectively, although the word "cure" might not always apply. Treatment often involves a combination of therapies, medications, and support. While some individuals may experience full recovery, others may need ongoing support to manage their symptoms and lead fulfilling lives. It's essential for individuals facing mental health challenges to seek professional help to determine the most appropriate treatment for their specific condition.

    - what are common symptoms of mental illness ?

    Common symptoms of mental diseases include feeling very sad or worried, having trouble sleeping, eating too much or too little, and difficulty concentrating. Some people might avoid friends and activities they used to enjoy. Physical symptoms like headaches or stomachaches can also happen. Everyone is different, so symptoms vary. If someone notices these changes in how they feel or act, it's important to talk to someone they trust or a professional. Getting support, like talking to a counselor or doctor, can help understand and manage these symptoms, leading to better mental well-being.

    - does every mental state makes you insane ?

    No, not every mental state makes a person "insane." Mental health exists on a spectrum, and experiencing challenges doesn't mean someone is insane. Many people face stress, sadness, or anxiety at some point, and these feelings are part of being human. "Insanity" is a legal term and doesn't accurately describe mental health. Most individuals, even those with mental health conditions, can lead fulfilling lives with support and treatment. It's crucial to seek understanding and empathy, recognizing that mental health is diverse, and many people successfully manage and cope with their unique experiences.

    - is there a link between social media usage and increased feelings of loneliness ?

    Yes, there is a link between social media usage and increased feelings of loneliness. Excessive time on social platforms may lead to social comparison, anxiety, and a sense of inadequacy. Seeing others' curated and seemingly perfect lives can contribute to feelings of isolation. Real-world social interactions are essential for well-being, and an overemphasis on virtual connections can hinder genuine relationships. Finding a balance between online and offline interactions is crucial to maintaining positive mental health, as loneliness is often alleviated through meaningful face-to-face connections and a more realistic perspective on social media content.
