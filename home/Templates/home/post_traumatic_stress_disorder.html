POST TRAUMATIC DISORDER

It is a mental health condition that can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. Individuals with PTSD may encounter persistent, distressing thoughts and memories, impacting their daily lives. Coping with PTSD is a journey towards healing and resilience.

Symptoms:
  • -Intrusive Memories: Recurrent, distressing memories, or flashbacks of the traumatic event.
  • -Avoidance: Avoidance of reminders, thoughts, or feelings associated with the trauma.
  • -Negative Changes in Mood: Persistent negative emotions, feeling detached, or a diminished interest in activities.
  • -Changes in Reactivity: Heightened arousal, including irritability, difficulty sleeping, or being easily startled.
  • -Cognition and Mood Symptoms: Negative changes in beliefs or expectations about oneself or the world.
Remedies:
  • -Grounding Techniques: Engage in activities that bring attention to the present moment to reduce anxiety.
  • -Expressive Writing: Journaling about the traumatic experience can be a therapeutic outlet.
  • -Self-Care: Prioritize self-care activities to promote physical and emotional well-being.
  • -Support Groups: Connecting with others who have experienced similar traumas can provide a sense of understanding and validation.
  • Mind-Body Techniques: Practices like mindfulness and yoga can aid in managing symptoms.
