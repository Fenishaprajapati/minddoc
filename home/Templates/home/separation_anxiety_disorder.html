SEPARATION ANXIETY DISORDER

Separation Anxiety Disorder is not just for kids. It's the distress experienced when separated from loved ones, leading to a constant fear of harm or loss.

Symptoms:
  • The symptoms range from avoiding being alone and school refusal to constant reassurance seeking.
  • 1.Excessive Distress: Feeling extremely upset when anticipating or experiencing separation.
  • 2.Nightmares: Disturbing dreams related to separation. 3.Physical Complaints: Headaches, stomachaches, or other physical symptoms.
  • 4.Avoidance of Being Alone: Constantly seeking company to avoid separation. 5.School Refusal: Resistance or refusal to attend school due to separation anxiety.
  • 6.Reassurance Seeking: Constantly seeking reassurance from loved ones.
Remedies:
  • -Gradual Exposure: Slowly adapting to separations to reduce distress. -Utilize Comfort Objects: Having a familiar item can provide security.
  • -Building a Support Network: Establishing connections for emotional support. -Professional Help: Seeking therapy if separation anxiety significantly affects daily life.
  • -Create Predictable Routines: Establishing consistency can provide comfort. -Gradual Separation Techniques: Slowly increasing the time spent apart.
Diagnosis:
  • - A mental health professional evaluates symptoms and the impact on daily functioning to diagnose Separation Anxiety Disorder.
Here are few fun facts:
  • - Puppies can experience separation anxiety too; your furry friend might miss you as much as you miss them.
  • - The longest recorded case of separation anxiety in humans lasted 53 years, with twins who were separated at birth.
