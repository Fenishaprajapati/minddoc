SOCIAL ANXIETY DISORDER

SAD is the fear of judgment, making social situations an emotional battleground. It's like having stage fright on a constant loop, fearing embarrassment or humiliation in social interactions.

Symptoms:
  • 1.SAD turns social interactions into an emotional battlefield. Picture intense fear, trembling, and a persistent fear of judgment.
  • 2.Intense Fear of Social Situations: Overwhelming anxiety in social settings.
  • 3.Avoidance of Social Interactions: Steer clear of gatherings or events. 4.Physical Symptoms: Blushing, trembling, sweating in social situations.
  • 5.Nausea or Upset Stomach: Anxiety can affect the digestive system. 6.Tremors: Involuntary trembling in social situations.
  • 7.Self-Consciousness: Intense self-awareness in social interactions.
Remedies:
  • -Our toolkit includes role-playing with friends, positive affirmations to combat negative thoughts, and the art of mindfulness in social settings.
  • -Gradual Exposure: Face social situations gradually to build tolerance. -Therapy: Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) can help change negative thought patterns.
  • -Self-Compassion Exercises: Being kind to oneself and challenging self-critical thoughts. -Role-Playing: Practice social situations with a trusted friend.
  • -Positive Affirmations: Challenge negative thoughts with positive self-talk. -Mindfulness in Social Settings: Focus on the present moment.
Diagnosis:
  • - A mental health professional evaluates social fears and their impact on daily life.
Here are few fun facts:
  • - Butterflies in your stomach aren't reserved for excitement; they can also flutter with anxiety.
  • - The fear of public speaking, a common social anxiety trigger, is called glossophobia.
